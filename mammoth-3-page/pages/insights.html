Paleontological Finding

Paleontological analysis of the Huntington Mammoth revealed that the specimen was a Columbian Mammoth, estimated to be around 65 years old at the time of its death. The contents of its stomach showed a diet consisting primarily of fir needles and twigs, which helped scientists better understand its natural habitat and feeding habits. Further examination of the skeleton indicated signs of arthritis and severely worn teeth, suggesting the mammoth experienced age-related health issues common among older individuals of the species.

Info at a Glance:

  • Species: Columbian Mammoth
  • Age: 65 years old
  • Diet: Fir needles and twigs
  • Health: Evidence of arthritis, worn teeth
