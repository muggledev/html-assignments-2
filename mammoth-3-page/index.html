Overview

In August 1988, construction worker Chris Nielson discovered a massive bone during dam reconstruction near Huntington Reservoir, leading to the discovery of a nearly complete Columbian mammoth skeleton.

Discovery Details

  • Date: August 8, 1988
  • Location: Huntington Reservoir, Utah
  • Elevation: 9,000 ft
  • Significance: Highest elevation mammoth discovery in North America

Excavation and Preservation

Paleontologists from Utah State University and volunteers recovered over 90% of the skeleton. The boggy environment preserved the bones and even the mammoth's stomach contents (fir needles and twigs).
